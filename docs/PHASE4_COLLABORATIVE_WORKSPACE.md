# Phase 4 — Collaborative Academic Workspace

Phase 4 governs people, authority, institutional policy and submission responsibility around the canonical thesis.

## Non-negotiable authority model

- The student remains the author and owns acceptance of academic-text suggestions.
- Supervisors review, comment, suggest, issue instructions and approve academic content against immutable review snapshots.
- Formatting operators may correct structure, metadata and governed presentation, but may not silently rewrite argumentation.
- Department and institution administrators manage membership, assignments, policy and workflow metadata; manuscript-content access is separately granted.
- External reviewers receive time-limited, revocable, version-bound read-only access.
- Robofox remains an assistant and never gains human approval authority.

## Delivery sequence

1. Multi-tenant institutions, departments, memberships, invitations and capability checks.
2. Snapshot-bound review cycles, anchored comments and structured human suggestions.
3. Separate academic, citation, formatting, institutional and submission approvals with dependency-aware invalidation.
4. Supervisor/operator queues, assignments, handoffs, instructions, notifications and human-readable audit events.
5. Versioned institutional policies, profiles and official templates with staging and impact analysis.
6. Sealed submission packages, attestations, withdrawals, post-viva revisions and external-review grants.
7. Retention, export/deletion workflows, support access controls and aggregate non-surveillance analytics.

This branch remains isolated from Phase 3 and will not be merged or deployed until dedicated tenant-isolation and inherited regression suites pass.
